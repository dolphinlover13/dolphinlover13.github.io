Travel to Mantua, Italy

Why should you Visit Mantua?

Mantua is an ancient Italian city which is well over 4,000 years old and has a whopping history too. Mantua is a city surrounded by 3 artificial lakes in the northern Italian region and Mantua was declared the Italian Capital of Culture in 2016.

3 Best Places to visit in Mantua:

  • Palazzo Ducale

    • As big as seven football fields, the massive castle has over 500 rooms. It was ruled by the Gonzaga family, one of the most powerful families in Europe during the Middle Ages.

  • Piazza Sordello

    • It is the oldest square in Mantua, and was probably the location of the Etruscan town.

  • Basilica di Sant’Andrea

    • Built way back in the 15th Century this beautiful basilica too over 300 years to complete! It’s said that the basilica holds a vile of holy blood that was identified as authentic almost 1,000 years ago.

    Things to do and eat in Mantua:

  1. Watch the sunset over Mantua...

    2. Just across the bridge, and over the water from, Piazza Sordello is one of the best views of the city. Head across around sunset and watch the city transform from day to night.

  2. Evening Show in Teatro Bibiena...

    3. The Bibiena Theater was built in 1767…entirely out of wood. It was used to host not just concerts and plays but also scientific experiments.The theater hosts both daytime visits and evening shows today.

  3. Eat Cotechino...

    4. Made with a filling of rind, bacon and different seasonings and cooked for an extra long time, cotechino is traditionally served with lentils to celebrate the New Year, but the hearty, simple dish is delicious anytime.
